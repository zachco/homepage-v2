Enabling extended data usage through experience and performance improvements


Project Overview

Within the Integrate platform today there are many pages with poor performance. The main cause as to why these pages are slow to load can be tied back to the Lists feature, and the architecture of List data.

Lists are used to control and standardize incoming lead data, typically by excluding or targeting certain emails, companies or general criteria like country, state or job title. In the Lists page you can view a table of all the lists in your account and perform actions like creating or editing lists, and configuring list mapping.

In addition to causing performance issues across the application, there are many workflow issues with Lists that cause unnecessary and manual work for users. Improving this part of our application will save users large amounts of time, improve page load times, and keep moving our product in a world-class direction.

Challenge

Performance-based issues:

  • Roughly half of all page load times are currently slow because of Lists
  • A lack of bulk operations causes a lot of extra time for internal and external customers alike. When list changes need to occur across many sources, users today must manually make each change one at a time.
  • Sorting and organizing lists are not possible today, causing a messy environment in a vital place of our platform.
  • Lists touch many parts of the application today, creating an architecture that will become slower and slower as time moves on.

UX-based issues:

  • Bulk operation: Applying lists to sources is a monotonous task that requires the user to add them one by one.
  • Deleting, archiving, and organizing lists: Once the “Lists” page loads, there are other issues that cause workflow pains.
  • Maintenance of frequently used lists: Suppression Lists are commonly used and maintained within Eloqua and Marketo.
  • Uploading large lists: There is no easy way for users to upload very large lists into our platform today.

Role and Team

I served as the sole designer and researcher, working with a team of one product manager and 5 software engineers.

Impact and Opportunities

Metrics tracked:

  1. Average page load time across the whole application
    • Anticipated improvement of 25-33%
    • The average page load time when opening a source decreased from 7+ seconds to ~1-2 seconds.
  2. Time saved for CS and customers. The bulk operations that the new Lists UI will have will save CS time when having to make many changes to sources at once.
