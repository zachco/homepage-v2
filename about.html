About Me

I’ve been interested in human behavior ever since I was a child. I love to learn about people and why they do what they do.

Human-Centered Design and Research came as a natural progression for me, because my interests in design and motivation became users and their interactions with the systems and things they use every day.

My professional work has been focused on complex, enterprise applications. I enjoy tackling these challenges as I have the opportunity to design beyond the screen, and collaborate with areas of the entire product team, from strategy and research to implementation and continued growth.

I'm also a volunteer with the Emergency Design Collective, working to provide responses to urgent public health crises through multidisciplinary collaboration, design, and research.

In my spare time, I enjoy writing, reading, and photography.
